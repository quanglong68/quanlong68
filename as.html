Terrestrial Animals

Terrestrial animals are creatures that live on land, thriving in diverse habitats like forests, deserts, grasslands, and mountains. They are adapted to their environments with unique features that help them survive and thrive.

Famous Animals:

Elephants:

The elephant is the largest land animal on Earth. Known for their intelligence and memory, elephants use their trunks to eat, drink, and interact with their environment. They are found in forests and savannas.

MAIN FOODS ARE:

  1. Grass
  2. Trees, leaves, tree bark
  3. Fruits (such as bananas, watermelon)

Lion:

Often called the "king of the jungle," lions are powerful predators that live in prides. They are known for their loud roars and majestic manes, mainly inhabiting grasslands and open plains.

Kangaroo:

Native to Australia, kangaroos are marsupials that use their strong hind legs to hop quickly across vast distances. They are herbivores and often seen in open fields or forests.
